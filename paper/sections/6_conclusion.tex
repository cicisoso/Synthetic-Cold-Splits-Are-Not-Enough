\section{Conclusion}

We presented a validation-oriented study of distribution shift in DTI prediction. Across a five-model comparison panel, a 3-seed synthetic reference panel on \texttt{BindingDB\_Kd} showed that cold-start regimes are already heterogeneous, while temporal/provenance benchmarks built from patent and non-patent BindingDB subsets changed the ranking again. Additional scaffold-restricted and overlap-restricted analyses further showed that conclusions within the temporal family depend on overlap policy. Year-band and overlap-bucket analyses indicated that real OOD behavior is structured rather than monolithic. Overall, these results show that synthetic cold-start and temporal/provenance benchmarks are not interchangeable validation settings for DTI prediction. Future DTI studies should therefore report both synthetic and temporal/provenance evidence when making model-generalization claims and when selecting models for practical chemoinformatics workflows.
