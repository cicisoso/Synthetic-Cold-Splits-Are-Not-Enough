\subsection{Additional real-OOD support benchmarks}

The main paper focuses on the strongest evidence chain: the synthetic \texttt{BindingDB\_Kd} reference panel, the primary temporal/provenance benchmarks, and the patent year/overlap diagnosis. \texttt{BindingDB\_Ki} and \texttt{DAVIS} remain supporting real-OOD benchmarks: both now use matched 3-seed evaluation, but they reinforce rather than replace the temporal story. To keep the support analysis focused on the most decision-relevant axes, the table reports the strongest pooled language-model baselines rather than rerunning the full five-model panel.

\input{tables/table_support_benchmarks}

On the \texttt{BindingDB\_Ki} support split, Table~\ref{tab:support} shows that \texttt{base} remains ahead of both \texttt{DTI-LM} and \texttt{HyperPCM}. On \texttt{DAVIS}, \texttt{base} and \texttt{DTI-LM} are nearly tied on mean \AUPRC{}, but \texttt{base} stays slightly higher on the paper's primary ranking metric. Together, these support panels reinforce the main message that strong recent baselines do not transfer uniformly across OOD definitions.

\subsection{Additional robustness analyses}

Table~\ref{tab:robustness-probes} records the additional analyses used to test whether the main conclusion depends on provenance or overlap policy. The first analysis constructs a non-patent temporal benchmark directly from the official BindingDB release. The second replaces entity holdout with a Murcko-scaffold holdout. We further add two controlled patent variants that explicitly suppress recurrent train pairs or seen-drug overlap. These analyses do not erase the benchmark-sensitivity finding. Instead, they sharpen it: under \texttt{patent\_temporal\_pair\_novel} and \texttt{patent\_temporal\_drug\_novel}, the AUPRC winner changes again, with \texttt{DTI-LM} overtaking \texttt{RF} while \texttt{RF - base} becomes unreliable on the pair-novel variant.

\input{tables/table_robustness_probes}

\subsection{Paired uncertainty support}

Table~\ref{tab:bootstrap-summary} summarizes the paired bootstrap intervals for the five-model comparison panel. The strongest supported deltas still include \texttt{RF} on synthetic \texttt{unseen\_drug}, \texttt{blind\_start}, and the aggregate patent and non-patent temporal benchmarks. At the same time, the controlled patent rows show why the paper should not overstate the temporal story. \texttt{RF - base} on \texttt{patent\_pair\_novel} crosses zero, while \texttt{DTI-LM - RF} is strictly positive on both controlled patent variants. By contrast, the synthetic \texttt{unseen\_target} comparison \texttt{RF - base} also crosses zero, and the scaffold-split \texttt{HyperPCM - base} comparison crosses zero as well, which is exactly why those rows are described conservatively in the main text.

\subsection{Method-centric ablations}

Earlier project stages explored routers, conservative target-memory variants, and selective fallback rules. These analyses were useful for method diagnosis, but they do not define the final paper narrative. The benchmark paper is intentionally centered on ranking instability across shift definitions rather than on proposing a new best-performing method.
